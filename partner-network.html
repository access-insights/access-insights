How partners strengthen the network.

Who partners are

Partners include organizations, independent professionals, funders, academic groups, community providers, companies, startups, and site partners. Site partners are communities or service environments where research can include the perspectives of people who live, work, receive support, and make operational decisions there.

Living Labs site partners may include independent living communities, assisted living communities, and similar settings where resident, staff, and leadership perspectives help reveal how solutions work in real life.

What partners contribute

  • Expertise that helps shape stronger research questions and more practical solution paths.
  • Funding, capacity, or infrastructure that helps promising opportunities move from insight to execution.
  • Community insights, referrals, sites, and lived-context knowledge that make research more representative.
  • Reach and delivery pathways that help validated solutions connect with the people who need them.

What partners receive

  • Opportunities to participate in research operations or commercial scaling pathways.
  • Access to quantitative research insights from relevant projects and surveys.
  • Visibility into prioritized project opportunities they can follow or contribute to.
  • Collaboration with aligned organizations working to create meaningful impact.
