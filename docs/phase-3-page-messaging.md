# Phase 3 Page-Level Messaging

Last updated: 2026-06-13

## Direction

This artifact turns the approved Phase 2 sitemap into page-level messaging. It is not final website copy yet; it is the working content structure for drafting and implementation.

Global messaging constraints:

- Position Access Insights as research innovation infrastructure.
- Center human variability and the full spectrum of human experience.
- Keep disability, accessibility, and aging as core strengths without making the site feel disability-only.
- Appeal implicitly to mission-aligned funders through credibility, scale, governance, community value, and project momentum.
- Clearly communicate bespoke paid research availability.
- Do not ask directly for funding.
- Do not imply participant data is sold or made available to partners.
- Maintain an accessible, plain-language, high-trust tone.

## 1. Home

- Establish the core frame.
- Route visitors quickly to Platform, Programs, Research Services, Lab & Insights, and Contact.
- Make Access Insights feel credible, human-centered, and operationally mature.

Primary audiences:

- Strategic partners and funders.
- Corporate/product research buyers.
- Participants and partner organizations.
- Ecosystem allies.

Hero H1:

- Technology is moving faster than ever. Human experience is not.

Hero support:

Primary CTA:

- Explore the platform.

Secondary CTAs:

- Join the participant program.
- Join the partner network.
- Start a research conversation.

Sections:

1. Hero.
   - Explain the gap between technological acceleration and real human experience.
   - Avoid fear-based language; emphasize better grounding, better insight, better adoption.

2. Infrastructure value proposition.
   - Access Insights connects participants, partners, research operations, consent, governance, and insight generation.
   - The platform makes inclusive research more repeatable, credible, and actionable.

3. Programs entry.
   - Participant Program: contribute lived experience and help shape better products.
   - Partner Network: contribute referrals, sites, expertise, distribution, and strategic collaboration.

4. Research platform preview.
   - Registration, readiness, consent, partner onboarding, research workflows, surveys, reporting, and future intelligence.

5. Research services preview.
   - Living Labs, discovery, validation, co-design, community surveys, pilots, and bespoke research.

6. Lab & Insights preview.
   - Current Projects.
   - Proto-Lab.
   - Articles & Insights.

7. Team credibility preview.
   - Preserve the team’s depth and credibility without making the homepage too bio-heavy.

8. Contact band.
   - Invite strategic collaboration or research conversations.

Content risks:

- The homepage must not become abstract. It needs concrete routes and examples.
- Avoid making the hero sound like a generic AI consultancy.
